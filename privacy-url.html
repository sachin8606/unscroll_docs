Unscroll Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Unscroll ("the app") helps you set daily time limits on apps you choose and shows focus alerts when you reach those limits. This policy explains what information the app uses and how it is handled.

Summary

  • No account or sign-in required
  • Data is stored on your device, not on our servers
  • We do not sell your personal information
  • You control which apps are tracked and can turn protection off anytime

Information the app uses

On your device, Unscroll may store and use:

  • Apps you select for tracking and daily time limits you set
  • Usage time in those apps (via Android Usage access)
  • Focus scores, limit alerts, and optional day history (up to 90 days)
  • Optional profile fields you enter (such as your name and focus goal) for the dashboard and alerts

Permissions

To work as intended, Unscroll may ask for:

  • Usage access — to measure time spent in apps you choose to track
  • Display over other apps — to show limit alerts when you exceed a daily cap
  • Accessibility service — to detect which app is in the foreground for timely alerts (only if you enable it)
  • Notifications — for the foreground service that runs focus protection in the background

You can revoke these permissions in Android system settings at any time.

What we do not collect

  • We do not operate a cloud account for Unscroll
  • We do not receive your app usage on our servers by default
  • We do not sell or rent your data to third parties

Third parties

The app is distributed through Google Play, which has its own privacy practices. Unscroll does not include third-party advertising SDKs in the app.

Children

Unscroll is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top will change when we do.
